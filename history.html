History of Miami Florida

Fun Facts

Miami was named after one of its Native American tribes. The city's name comes from the Mayaimi tribe, who were indigenous people that lived in the area. The word "Mayaimi" means "big water," which was a reference to Lake Okeechobee.
Miami was the only major U.S. city founded by a woman: Julia Tuttle is known as the "Mother of Miami" because she convinced a wealthy businessman, Henry Flagler, to extend his railroad to Miami. This helped to turn the small settlement into a major city.
Miami's Art Deco district has the largest collection of Art Deco architecture in the world: The Art Deco district in Miami Beach has over 800 buildings that were built between the 1920s and 1940s. The district is known for its pastel colors, neon signs, and geometric shapes.
